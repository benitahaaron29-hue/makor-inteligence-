Dollar firm, front-end USTs bid into US CPI as desks de-risk into the print.

Overnight price action leaned risk-on, with the dollar bid against the funders and the belly of the UST curve receiving real-money flow ahead of a 3.1 % forecast. The desk maintains a tactical bias to fade USD strength on confirmation of disinflation.

By A. Benitah · Macro & FX Strategy Published 06:30 GMT · 11 May 2026 ~ 4 min read  Live
§ 01

Executive Summary

Good morning. Overnight price action heading into the 11 May session leaned risk-on. The dollar is firm against the funders, real-money is receiving the belly of the UST curve, and equity vol is offered. The key event is the US CPI print at 12:30 GMT, forecast at 3.1 % YoY (vs 3.2 % prior) with core MoM forecast at +0.3 %.

We maintain a tactical bias to fade USD strength on confirmation of disinflation, while watching credit and front-end vol for early dislocation signals. Positioning is moderately long USD into the print; a soft surprise would force unwind into thin liquidity.

§ 02 · FX

G10 & Crosses

EUR/USD held a tight 30-pip range overnight, with leveraged accounts paring shorts ahead of US CPI. GBP/USD remains pinned by sticky services inflation and an unconvincing BoE dovish pivot — 1.27 resistance still intact. USD/JPY drifted lower as the front-end of UST richens; intervention risk increases above 152.50.

AUD/USD continues to trade as a China proxy; copper strength supportive but iron-ore weakness caps upside. The desk likes selling USDCHF rallies into 0.9050.

§ 03 · Rates

USTs & G7 Curves

Front-end USTs rallied 4bp on dovish Fed-speak; 2s/10s steepening resumed with real-money receivers in the belly. Bunds tracked USTs but underperformed at the long end on supply concession.

JGB 10y stalled near 0.95 % — BoJ presence keeps the curve pinned. Gilts continued to lag with GBP rates pricing a shallower BoE easing cycle than peers. Watch 2y UST auction tail today as a positioning gauge.

§ 04 · Equities

Index & Single-Stock Dynamics

S&P futures flat overnight after a marginal new high in the cash session. Semis led on AI capex commentary; defensives lagged. Europe opens mixed with luxury under pressure on China demand concerns. Nikkei firmer on a softer JPY but breadth narrow.

Earnings dispersion remains the dominant single-stock driver — index-level vol compressed, single-name vol elevated.

§ 05 · Commodities

Energy, Metals, Bulks

Brent traded a $1 range as geopolitical risk premium leaked out of the curve; spec longs trimmed but structural backwardation intact. Gold consolidated near $2,330 with real yields the swing factor into CPI.

Copper firm on tightening concentrate market; LME stocks at multi-year lows. Nat gas remains a regional story with European storage adequate heading into shoulder season.
